# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Australian-based Small and Medium Businesses (SMBs). These are often non-technical owners or managers looking for cybersecurity solutions that don't feel overwhelming or "too techy."

## Product Purpose
To provide accessible, localized cybersecurity services. Success looks like an Australian business owner feeling that their digital presence is as secure as a safe harbour, moving away from the fear-based marketing common in the industry.

## Brand Personality
Local, Understandable, Safe. The brand uses a "safe harbour/shoreline" motif to evoke a sense of protection and local grounding. It should feel professional yet approachable.

## Anti-references
Avoid "hacker" clichés (matrix code, hooded figures, neon green on black). Avoid cold, overly corporate stock-photo-driven security sites.

## Design Principles
- **The Safe Harbour**: Use nautical and shoreline motifs (waves, headlands, horizons) to reinforce the feeling of a protected perimeter.
- **Clarity over Complexity**: Use simple, direct language and clear visual hierarchies to make security feel "understandable" rather than opaque.
- **Local Grounding**: The aesthetic should feel relevant to the Australian context—bright, airy (in light mode), and sturdy.
- **Technical Quietness**: Security should feel like a background utility that just works, not an aggressive or loud presence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance.
- High contrast for readability, especially for users who may be multitasking or in varying lighting conditions.
- Reduced motion options for the wave animations.
